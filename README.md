# Mississippi-University-Scholarship

## Project Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze alumni donation patterns to identify key donor segments and support data-driven fundraising strategies.


## Dataset
https://github.com/anmolsodhi848-wq/Mississippi-University-Scholarship/blob/main/Mississipi%20University%20Scholarship.xlsx


## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

1. **Total Gift Amount** ‚Äì Overall donations collected across all alumni.
2. **Average Gift Amount per Donor** ‚Äì Mean contribution value to assess donor generosity.
3. **Donor Count** ‚Äì Number of unique alumni who made contributions.
4. **Allocation-wise Contribution** ‚Äì Donation totals segmented by allocation subcategory or gift purpose.
5. **Year-wise Donation Trend** ‚Äì Gift amounts tracked across graduation years or donation years.
6. **Top Contributing Segments** ‚Äì Highest donations by city, state, major, or college.


## Process Description

1. **Data Cleaning in Excel:** Removed duplicates, standardized text fields, formatted dates, and validated gift amounts to ensure data accuracy.
2. **Data Structuring:** Organized fields such as allocation category, demographics, academic details, and gift information for easy analysis.
3. **Dashboard Development in Tableau:** Imported cleaned data, created calculated fields, and built interactive charts to visualize donor trends and segment contributions.
4. **Insight Generation:** Analyzed patterns across demographics, majors, colleges, and allocation categories to identify high-value donors and fundraising opportunities.

## Conclusions

1. **Science and Engineering are Key Donor Bases:** The College of Natural Science consistently has the highest number of recipients (496), and the Engineering/No Major category contributes the largest single gift amount ($562,519). This indicates that the science and engineering fields are critical both for student support and financial contributions.
2. **West and Southwest States Dominate Giving:** California, Texas, and Colorado are the top three states, contributing over $8.5 million combined. This highlights a geographic concentration of major donors in the Western and Southwestern U.S.
3. **Recent Alumni (1990s-2000s) are Peak Donors:** Graduates from the 1990 and 2000 cohorts account for the largest gift spikes in the Graduation Year trend, with the 2000 cohort showing the highest total ($1,659,628). This suggests that cultivation efforts should prioritize these alumni groups.
4. **Annual Funds and Diversity are Primary Allocation Targets:** A large portion of the funds are directed toward the University Annual Fund ($2,492,738) and the Diversity Fund ($2,656,507), showing a strong institutional focus on general operations and diversity initiatives.


üöÄ Future Scope

1. **Geographic Expansion:** Target development efforts to capitalize on high-potential states like CA, TX, and CO, while also analyzing growth opportunities in emerging markets.
2. **Cohort Optimization:** Prioritize engagement and major gift solicitation from the 1990 and 2000 graduation cohorts as they represent the highest current giving capacity.
3. **Programmatic Growth:** Leverage the existing strength of the College of Natural Science (high recipients) and the Engineering major (high gift amount) for potential campaign initiatives.
4. **Fund Development:** Analyze the sustained demand for the Diversity Fund and University Annual Fund to determine potential for endowment growth and annual fund goals.


üí° Recommendations
1. **Implement Targeted Digital Campaigns:** Launch specialized social and email campaigns for the 1990s-2000s alumni focusing on impact stories related to their college/major.
2. **Strengthen Major Gift Officer Deployment:** Reallocate resources to focus on the West and Southwest regions to service the concentration of high-value donors in CA, TX, and CO.
3. **Improve Stewardship for Core Funds:** Enhance personalized reporting and thank-you communications for donors contributing to the Diversity Fund and Faculty Chair Endowments to ensure retention and upgrades.
4. **Maximize Giving Seasonality:** Plan significant outreach and matching challenges during the high-activity months of March, June, September, and December.
